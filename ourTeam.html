Christine Henshaw

Grant Manager, United States and Canada

The third generation of a family of grant writers, Christine Paveling began her career early, while completing her studies at the University of Kansas in 2004. Her first grant proposal, a $285,000 request for a new school library in North Bolivar, MS, was also her first success. The project was highlighted in the grant guidelines the following year.

Upon graduating with a B.A. in English Literature, Christine had built a successful and growing grant consultancy business specializing in U.S. government and foundation funding. She has over a decade of experience working with individuals and organizations to find and secure funding for their projects. A move to Ottawa, Ontario in 2011 enabled Christine to broaden her grants knowledge beyond the United States to Canada. To date, she has secured over $53,000,000 in grant funding for her clients.

Christine joined the LDA team in 2017 as the Grants Resource Manager for U.S. and Canada. Passionate about writing, research, and philanthropy, and with cross-border grants experience, the job was a natural fit. Her favorite projects are those that use innovative approaches to improve the lives of people in underserved communities, particularly children.

After four very cold, very long Ontario winters, Christine informed her husband that they were moving to Austin, TX where they currently live with their two daughters, an ill- tempered cat, and two enormous dogs.
